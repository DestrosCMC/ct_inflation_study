2022 CT Employee Wage Growth vs Inflation

This second slide displays the 2022 wages vs the inflation rate. The inflation rate used to calculate these salaries (2021) is less than the 2022 rate (4.7% vs 8%).
However, this is still a large margin above the 2% that the Federal Reserve aims to keep.

Select between Real 2022 Wage and Projected 2022 Wage from 2021 Inflation and 2021 Wage

However, as you explore the scene, you may see that wages are close to the inflation tracking values. This finding is interesting and supports the hypothesis that anticipatory wage increases can drive inflation even higher.

The data for this study comes from CT Data and the State Employee Payroll Data CT Data
The inflation data comes from the Federal Reserve of St. Louis FRED
